Moderation Commands

The Basics

The first part of a command ( the word after the / ) is referred to as just the command or command name. I bet you are gasping in surprise at this revelation.

Other parts after this are called arguments. Sometimes, you can run a command without some of its arguments, like not adding reason to a /mute for example. These arguments are optional arguments, and they are shown by [square brackets], while required arguments are shown by <chevrons>.

Player Notes - for suspicious players

  • If you believe a player may have broken the rules, or intends to break the rules, be sure to add a note on them for yourself and other staff to keep track of suspicious players.
  • /addnote <player name> <note>
    Adds a note to a player. Some examples of what you might put here would be: “Possible x-ray” or “Suspicious PvP - possible client”. This makes sure that other staff can keep track of this, and help them make decisions about bans in the future.
  • /notes <player name> 
    View the notes on one player.
  • /notes
    View the notes on all online players.
  • /bmclear <player name> notes
    Head staff only, ask head staff if you want this to be executed. Removes all notes from a player.

Using the reports system

  • The reports system is very useful for keeping track of player accusations etc.
    Each report has a number - its ID. Keep this in mind whilst reading this section. 
  • /report <player name> <reason>
    Normal players should use this, as well as non-moderating staff.
  • /reports
    Shows a list of the latest reports, with their status, time and player. You can click on reports in chat to get more information. The report id is shown at the start of each report.
  • /reports assign
    Assign a report to yourself, so other staff know you are working on it.
  • /reports assign <playername>
    For head mod or higher. Assign a report to another staff member.
  • /reports close <id> [comment]
    Close a report once you have finished and taken action. You can add a comment on what you did so that other staff know what happened in the future.

Investigating and warning players

  • /axr
    Open anti-xray menu (Only on stream server as of 20/02/2021).
  • /axr orecount ores <player name>
    Shows a menu with how many ores the player has mined.
  • /axr orecount clear <player name>
    Clears a player's ore count. I don’t really know why you’d want to do this to be honest. Maybe if you get a false positive xray alert.
  • /warn <player name> [reason]
    Warn a player that they are breaking the rules.
  • /dwarn <player name>
    Deletes the last warning that a player was given. Useful if you realise you made a mistake etc.
  • /bminfo <player name>
    Tells you information about previous warnings, bans, mutes and kicks. Also tells you other usernames that use the same IP address, which are possible alt accounts. You can also use this to find when they last joined the server. To see specific info about warnings or bans a player has had add -w or -b to the end of the command.
  • /bmactivity <time> [playername]
    Check recent bans, mutes and kicks. Optionally focus on one player.
  • /v
    Quickly become invisible/visible in creative mode. Useful when watching players.
  • /co i
    Enable core inspect mode. Things you should know:

    - Right clicking on chests shows you what was taken/placed in them by who.

    - Right clicking on blocks shows you what blocks were placed on the face of the block you clicked on. E.g. right clicking on the floor tells you was was placed on top of the floor.

    - Left clicking on blocks shows you who placed them.
  • /co lookup <player name> time:<how long in the past> action:[type of activity]
    Very useful. Lets you look up what a player has been doing on the server. The time argument is required, but the action is not. You can then use /co l <page number> to look through pages if there are a lot of entries. An example of how you might use this:

    If you think MattzMC might have stole something from a chest: 
    /co lookup MattzMC time:30m action:container 

    This would then give you a list of what that player has taken and placed in containers. 
  • /invsee <player name>
    Lets you see the inventory of a player, as well as confiscate items from them.
  • /echest <player name>
    Lets you open a player’s ender chest.
  • /otp <player name>
    Stands for offline teleport. Teleports to a player’s last location before leaving the server.

Stopping and moving players

  • /freeze <player name>
    Stops a player from moving temporarily. Do this if you see someone breaking the rules in front of you for example, while you investigate.
  • /unfreeze <playername>
    Unfreezes a player.
  • /tp <player name>
    Teleport to a player.
  • /tphere <player name>
    Teleport a player to you.

Temporary bans and mutes

  • Time can be specified with minutes (m), hours (h), days (d), weeks (w) and months (m). This is what should be used the majority of the time, especially for borderline rule violations. 
  • /tempmute <player name> <time> [reason]
    Mute a player temporarily. Always add a reason.
  • /tempban <player name <time> [reason]
    Ban a player temporarily. Always add a reason.
  • /kick <player name> [reason]
    Disconnects a player, but they can immediately rejoin. Useful if stream smp is full and there is a queue.

Permanent mutes and bans

  • Be careful permanently banning or muting users! They can be difficult for staff to keep track of.
  • /mute <player name> [reason]
    Mutes a player until they are manually unmuted. Don’t do this unless you have a good reason, because you will probably forget to unmute them later.
  • /ban <player name> [reason]
    Ban a player permanently. Always be absolutely sure before perm banning users. It is best to consult higher staff and/or getting a second opinion.

Rolling back damage and chests

  • To fix damage done by griefers/thieves
  • /co rollback user:<username> radius:<radius> time:<time>
    Will rollback the time you give, in a radius of what you specify. Example:

     /co rollback user:Systemax radius:2 time:2m 
    Rolls back all actions Systemax did within 2 blocks of you, from the past 2 minutes.
